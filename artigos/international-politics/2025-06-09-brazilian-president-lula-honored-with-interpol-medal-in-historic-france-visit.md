## Lula Receives Interpol Medal in France for Fighting Transnational Crime

O Brazilian President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva made history as the first Brazilian head of state to visit the headquarters of the International Criminal Police Organization (Interpol) in Lyon, France. During this landmark visit, Lula was awarded a prestigious medal of excellence by Interpol, recognizing Brazil’s significant contributions to the global fight against transnational crime. The ceremony, attended by high-ranking Interpol officials and Brazilian delegates, underscored the strengthening ties between Brazil and the international law enforcement agency. Additionally, Lula signed a Declaration of Intentions to deepen cooperation, marking a pivotal moment in Brazil’s commitment to tackling cross-border criminal activities.

## A Recognition of Brazil’s Efforts

The medal, presented by Ahmed Naser Al-Raisi, President of Interpol’s Executive Committee, was a testament to Brazil’s proactive role in addressing complex global security challenges. In his acceptance speech, Lula emphasized the evolving nature of transnational crime, likening criminal networks to “multinational corporations” that infiltrate governments, economies, and societies worldwide. He highlighted Brazil’s efforts to combat issues such as drug trafficking, cybercrime, and environmental crimes, which have far-reaching implications beyond national borders.

Lula’s administration has prioritized international cooperation to address these challenges. Since taking office in 2023, his government has worked to strengthen partnerships with global organizations like Interpol to enhance intelligence sharing, improve law enforcement training, and develop strategies to disrupt criminal networks. The Interpol medal not only acknowledges these efforts but also signals Brazil’s growing influence in global security discussions.

## A Historic Visit to Interpol Headquarters

The visit to Interpol’s headquarters in Lyon was a symbolic milestone. No Brazilian president had previously set foot in the organization’s global hub, making Lula’s presence a powerful statement of Brazil’s commitment to international law enforcement collaboration. The ceremony was attended by key figures, including Valdecy Urquiza, a Brazilian who serves as Interpol’s Secretary-General. In a reciprocal gesture, Lula conferred the Order of Rio Branco, in the rank of Grand Officer, upon Urquiza, recognizing his contributions to global policing and his role in elevating Brazil’s profile within Interpol.

The event was more than a ceremonial exchange of honors. It provided a platform for substantive discussions on how Brazil and Interpol can work together to address pressing global challenges. The Declaration of Intentions signed during the visit outlines a framework for enhanced cooperation, including joint operations, technology sharing, and capacity-building initiatives. These measures are particularly critical in areas such as combating illegal deforestation in the Amazon, a priority for Lula’s government, which has faced scrutiny over environmental crime.

## The Global Context of Transnational Crime

Lula’s remarks during the ceremony shed light on the broader context of transnational crime. He noted that criminal organizations operate with a level of sophistication and coordination that rivals legitimate multinational enterprises. These networks exploit globalization, leveraging technology and porous borders to engage in activities ranging from human trafficking to money laundering. For countries like Brazil, which grapple with domestic challenges such as organized crime and corruption, international partnerships are essential to disrupting these networks.

Brazil’s strategic position in South America makes it a key player in addressing transnational crime. The country shares borders with 10 nations, many of which are affected by drug trafficking routes and illegal trade. Additionally, Brazil’s vast Amazon rainforest has become a battleground for environmental crimes, with illegal logging and mining operations often linked to international criminal syndicates. By strengthening ties with Interpol, Brazil aims to bolster its capacity to address these issues while contributing to global efforts to promote security and justice.

## Lula’s Vision for Multilateral Cooperation

Throughout his presidency, Lula has championed multilateralism as a cornerstone of Brazil’s foreign policy. His visit to Interpol reflects this philosophy, emphasizing the need for collective action to tackle global challenges. In his speech, Lula called for greater solidarity among nations, arguing that no single country can combat transnational crime in isolation. He advocated for increased investment in technology and intelligence-sharing platforms to stay ahead of increasingly sophisticated criminal networks.

The Declaration of Intentions signed during the visit is a concrete step toward realizing this vision. It includes provisions for Brazil to participate in Interpol’s global initiatives, such as the I-GRIP program, which focuses on combating organized crime, and the Cybercrime Directorate, which addresses the growing threat of digital offenses. These collaborations are expected to enhance Brazil’s law enforcement capabilities while fostering trust and coordination with international partners.

## Valdecy Urquiza: A Brazilian Leader at Interpol

The presence of Valdecy Urquiza, Interpol’s Secretary-General, added a layer of national pride to the event. As the first Brazilian to hold this position, Urquiza has been instrumental in advocating for greater representation of developing nations within Interpol. His leadership has also facilitated closer ties between Brazil and the organization, paving the way for initiatives like the one formalized during Lula’s visit.

Lula’s decision to honor Urquiza with the Order of Rio Branco was a fitting tribute to his contributions. The award, one of Brazil’s highest honors, recognizes individuals who have made significant contributions to the country’s international standing. Urquiza’s role at Interpol has not only elevated Brazil’s profile but also demonstrated the country’s capacity to produce leaders capable of addressing global challenges.

## Looking Ahead: Brazil’s Role in Global Security

The Lyon visit marks a new chapter in Brazil’s relationship with Interpol and its broader engagement with global security issues. As transnational crime continues to evolve, Brazil’s commitment to international cooperation will be crucial in shaping effective responses. The agreements signed during the visit lay the groundwork for sustained collaboration, with potential benefits for both Brazil and the international community.

For Lula, the Interpol medal is both a personal and national achievement. It reflects his administration’s efforts to restore Brazil’s reputation as a responsible global actor while addressing domestic and international challenges. By prioritizing partnerships with organizations like Interpol, Lula is positioning Brazil as a leader in the fight against transnational crime, with implications for security, environmental protection, and economic stability.

The awarding of an Interpol medal to President Lula da Silva on June 9, 2025, is a milestone in Brazil’s journey toward greater involvement in global security. The historic visit to Lyon, the signing of a Declaration of Intentions, and the recognition of Valdecy Urquiza underscore Brazil’s commitment to combating transnational crime through multilateral cooperation. As Lula continues to advocate for collective action, Brazil is poised to play a pivotal role in shaping a safer, more just world. This moment in Lyon will be remembered not only for its symbolic significance but also for the tangible steps taken to strengthen Brazil’s partnership with Interpol.
