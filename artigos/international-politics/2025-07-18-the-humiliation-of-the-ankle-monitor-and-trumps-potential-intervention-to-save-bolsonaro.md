## Bolsonaro’s Ankle Monitor Humiliation and Trump’s Bid to Save Him | Dunapress Journal

### A Symbol of Humiliation

On July 18, 2025, former Brazilian President Jair Bolsonaro faced what he called a “suprema humilhação” (supreme humiliation) after being ordered by Brazil’s Supreme Court (STF) to wear an ankle monitor. This measure, imposed by Justice Alexandre de Moraes, came as part of an ongoing investigation into allegations of Bolsonaro’s involvement in a supposed coup attempt following the 2022 election. The ankle monitor, alongside restrictions like a nighttime curfew and a ban on social media, marks a significant escalation in the judicial scrutiny facing the polarizing ex-president. As Bolsonaro navigates this public and personal affront, speculation swirls about a potential intervention by his ally, U.S. President Donald Trump, whose recent threats of 50% tariffs on Brazilian goods have been linked to demands for halting Bolsonaro’s prosecution. This article delves into the implications of Bolsonaro’s humiliation, the judicial measures against him, and the possibility of Trump’s influence shaping his fate.[](https://diariodopoder.com.br/brasil-e-regioes/csa-brasil/suprema-humilhacao-diz-bolsonaro-sobre-tornozeleira)[](https://www.bbc.com/portuguese/articles/cgjge04v2pyo)

### The Ankle Monitor: A Public Shaming

The imposition of an ankle monitor on a former head of state is a rare and symbolically charged act. For Bolsonaro, a figure who has long cultivated an image of defiance and strength, the device represents a profound blow to his public persona. Speaking to the press after the monitor was installed, Bolsonaro repeatedly described the measure as a “supreme humiliation,” emphasizing his status as a 70-year-old former president and denying any intent to flee Brazil. The restrictions accompanying the ankle monitor are stringent: he is confined to his home from 7 p.m. to 7 a.m., barred from contacting diplomats or his son Eduardo Bolsonaro, and prohibited from using social media or approaching embassies. These measures, authorized by the STF and supported by the Federal Police (PF) and the Attorney General’s Office (PGR), aim to prevent any risk of flight or interference in ongoing investigations.[](https://noticias.uol.com.br/politica/ultimas-noticias/2025/07/18/bolsonaro-entrevista-apos-operacao-da-pf.htm)[](https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2025/07/ex-presidente-bolsonaro-e-alvo-de-operacao-da-policia-federal-sob-ordem-do-stf.shtml)[](https://carro.blog.br/bolsonaro-vai-ser-preso-alexandre-de-moraes-manda-ex-presidente-usar-tornozeleira-eletronica.html)

The public nature of these measures amplifies their impact. Images of Bolsonaro, escorted by police to install the monitor, circulated widely, fueling both outrage among his supporters and celebration among his critics. Social media erupted with memes, reflecting the polarized sentiment in Brazil—some saw it as justice for alleged crimes, while others viewed it as political persecution. For Bolsonaro, the ankle monitor is not just a legal constraint but a deliberate act to tarnish his image and weaken his political influence ahead of a trial that could lead to imprisonment.[](https://www.jornalopcao.com.br/ultimas-noticias/confira-compilado-dos-melhores-memes-sobre-tornozeleira-eletronica-de-bolsonaro-726629/)

### The Context: Allegations of a Coup and Political Persecution

Bolsonaro’s legal troubles stem from accusations that he orchestrated a plot to undermine the 2022 election, won by President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva. The STF’s investigations, particularly those led by Justice Moraes, point to evidence of a coordinated effort to challenge the electoral outcome, including the January 8, 2023, riots in Brasília. The discovery of $14,000 in U.S. dollars, R$8,000 in Brazilian reais, and a pen drive hidden in a bathroom during a police search of Bolsonaro’s home further fueled suspicions of obstruction of justice. Additionally, the STF alleges that Bolsonaro and his son Eduardo sought to pressure Brazil’s judiciary by leveraging international support, particularly from the United States.[](https://www.diariocarioca.com/politica/bolsonaro-tornozeleira-perseguicao/)[](https://www.brasildefato.com.br/2025/07/18/com-tornozeleira-bolsonaro-fala-sobre-operacao-da-pf-suprema-humilhacao/)[](https://fpabramo.org.br/focusbrasil/2025/07/18/pf-cumpre-mandados-na-casa-de-bolsonaro-ex-presidente-foi-levado-para-colocar-tornozeleira-eletronica/)

Bolsonaro and his allies have framed these actions as a politically motivated “witch hunt.” He denies the existence of a “coup plan” and argues that the investigations lack concrete evidence, describing them as “political” rather than judicial. His supporters, including allies in the Liberal Party (PL), echo this narrative, claiming the ankle monitor and other measures are attempts to silence a prominent opposition figure. The timing of the measures, just days after Trump’s tariff threats, has only intensified these claims, with Bolsonaro’s camp suggesting the judiciary is reacting to external pressure rather than pursuing justice.[](https://www.estadao.com.br/politica/eliane-cantanhede/muita-alegria-com-trump-virou-tornozeleira-e-suprema-humilhacao-de-bolsonaro/)[](https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2025/07/bolsonaro-chama-de-suprema-humilhacao-restricoes-determinadas-pelo-stf.shtml)[](https://www.diariocarioca.com/politica/aliados-reagem-a-tornozeleira-de-bolsonaro-com-narrativa-de-perseguicao-tratado-como-criminoso/)

### Trump’s Tariff Threats: A Diplomatic Gambit?

The involvement of U.S. President Donald Trump adds a dramatic international dimension to Bolsonaro’s predicament. On July 9, 2025, Trump announced a proposed 50% tariff on Brazilian goods, explicitly tying the measure to what he called a “witch hunt” against Bolsonaro. In a subsequent letter, Trump demanded an immediate end to Bolsonaro’s prosecution, accusing Brazil’s judiciary of political persecution. This move was interpreted by the STF and the Lula administration as an attempt to interfere with Brazil’s sovereignty, prompting Moraes to cite it as evidence of Bolsonaro’s efforts to subvert the judicial process through foreign influence.[](https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2025/07/ex-presidente-bolsonaro-e-alvo-de-operacao-da-policia-federal-sob-ordem-do-stf.shtml)[](https://g1.globo.com/mundo/noticia/2025/07/17/trump-publica-carta-para-bolsonaro-em-defesa-de-ex-presidente.ghtml)[](https://fpabramo.org.br/focusbrasil/2025/07/18/pf-cumpre-mandados-na-casa-de-bolsonaro-ex-presidente-foi-levado-para-colocar-tornozeleira-eletronica/)

Trump’s support for Bolsonaro is not new. The two leaders share ideological alignment and have maintained a close relationship since Bolsonaro’s presidency. Eduardo Bolsonaro, currently in the U.S., has been vocal in rallying support among American conservative circles, allegedly seeking to pressure Brazil’s judiciary through diplomatic and economic means. The STF’s decision to bar Bolsonaro from contacting Eduardo underscores concerns about their coordinated efforts to leverage U.S. influence. Trump’s tariff threats, coupled with statements from figures like U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio about visa restrictions for foreign officials involved in “censoring Americans,” have heightened tensions between Brazil and the U.S.[](https://www.bbc.com/portuguese/articles/cgjge04v2pyo)[](https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2025/07/ex-presidente-bolsonaro-e-alvo-de-operacao-da-policia-federal-sob-ordem-do-stf.shtml)[](https://www.bbc.com/portuguese/articles/cgjge04v2pyo)

Could Trump’s intervention save Bolsonaro from prison? The possibility hinges on diplomatic and political leverage. A full-scale trade war, triggered by tariffs, could pressure Brazil’s economy, which relies heavily on exports to the U.S. However, polls suggest that 72% of Brazilians view Trump’s tariffs as misguided, and Lula’s government has vowed to respond with reciprocal measures, signaling resistance to external pressure. Within Brazil, the STF’s independence and the strength of the evidence against Bolsonaro make a direct “rescue” unlikely. Even if Trump’s actions bolster Bolsonaro’s narrative of victimhood, they may not alter the judicial outcome, especially as Moraes accelerates the trial process.[](https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2025/07/ex-presidente-bolsonaro-e-alvo-de-operacao-da-policia-federal-sob-ordem-do-stf.shtml)[](https://carro.blog.br/bolsonaro-vai-ser-preso-alexandre-de-moraes-manda-ex-presidente-usar-tornozeleira-eletronica.html)

### The Global and Domestic Fallout

The convergence of Bolsonaro’s legal battles and Trump’s intervention has far-reaching implications. Domestically, it deepens Brazil’s political polarization. Bolsonaro’s supporters see the ankle monitor as proof of a biased judiciary, while his opponents view it as a victory for democratic accountability. The measures have also isolated Bolsonaro politically, with even right-wing governors distancing themselves as they grapple with the economic fallout of potential U.S. tariffs.[](https://fpabramo.org.br/focusbrasil/2025/07/18/pf-cumpre-mandados-na-casa-de-bolsonaro-ex-presidente-foi-levado-para-colocar-tornozeleira-eletronica/)[](https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2025/07/ex-presidente-bolsonaro-e-alvo-de-operacao-da-policia-federal-sob-ordem-do-stf.shtml)

Internationally, the situation strains Brazil-U.S. relations. Lula’s administration has called Trump’s tariffs “unacceptable blackmail,” emphasizing the need to protect Brazil’s judicial sovereignty. The controversy also highlights the growing influence of global far-right networks, as figures like Eduardo Bolsonaro seek to internationalize their cause. However, Trump’s support may backfire, as it has galvanized Lula’s base and boosted his approval ratings in key demographics, according to recent polls.[](https://www.bbc.com/portuguese/articles/cgjge04v2pyo)[](https://www.diariocarioca.com/politica/aliados-reagem-a-tornozeleira-de-bolsonaro-com-narrativa-de-perseguicao-tratado-como-criminoso/)[](https://www.estadao.com.br/politica/eliane-cantanhede/muita-alegria-com-trump-virou-tornozeleira-e-suprema-humilhacao-de-bolsonaro/)

### The Road Ahead: Prison or Pardon?

As Bolsonaro’s trial looms, the prospect of prison grows more real. If convicted of leading a coup attempt, he could face 12 to 40 years, though analysts suggest a lighter sentence is possible. The ankle monitor and other restrictions are designed to ensure he remains in Brazil and complies with the judicial process. Yet, Bolsonaro’s history—such as his brief stay at the Hungarian embassy in 2024—raises concerns about potential flight risks.[](https://www.bbc.com/portuguese/articles/c62zp0djm3yo)[](https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/poder/2025/07/ex-presidente-bolsonaro-e-alvo-de-operacao-da-policia-federal-sob-ordem-do-stf.shtml)

Trump’s intervention, while bold, faces limits. Economic pressure might sway public opinion or political actors, but the STF’s autonomy and the weight of evidence against Bolsonaro make a direct intervention, such as a pardon or asylum, improbable. Bolsonaro himself has expressed a desire to negotiate with Trump to resolve the tariff issue, but his restricted status complicates such efforts. Meanwhile, Eduardo’s activities in the U.S. continue to draw scrutiny, with the STF viewing them as attempts to undermine Brazil’s institutions.[](https://www.cartacapital.com.br/cartaexpressa/suprema-humilhacao-o-que-disse-jair-bolsonaro-apos-ter-instalado-tornozeleira-eletronica/)[](https://fpabramo.org.br/focusbrasil/2025/07/18/pf-cumpre-mandados-na-casa-de-bolsonaro-ex-presidente-foi-levado-para-colocar-tornozeleira-eletronica/)

### A Test of Democracy

The ankle monitor on Jair Bolsonaro is more than a legal tool—it’s a symbol of Brazil’s struggle to balance accountability and polarization. For Bolsonaro, it’s a personal and political humiliation, one he attributes to a concerted effort to silence him. Trump’s threats to wield economic power in his defense add a layer of international intrigue, raising questions about sovereignty and global influence. Yet, the outcome rests with Brazil’s judiciary and its commitment to upholding the rule of law. As the trial approaches, the world watches to see whether justice prevails or if external pressures reshape Brazil’s democratic path.
