## **Tragic Death of Cleriston Cunha Raises Questions about Justice in Brazil**

In a deeply troubling incident, **[Cleriston Pereira da Cunha](https://www.gazetadopovo.com.br/vida-e-cidadania/preso-atos-8-janeiro-brasilia-morre-presidio-papuda/)**, an innocent Brazilian citizen, has lost his life while in the custody of the Brazilian state. Imprisoned since January 8 for his involvement in protests earlier that month, Cleriston's case has raised concerns about the justice system and the actions of the Lula government.

Despite a previously issued release decision, the fate of Cleriston hinged on the deliberation of the rapporteur, Alexandre de Moraes. Regrettably, the awaited decision never materialized, leaving Cleriston in a precarious position, reminiscent of a death sentence. This delay has sparked accusations of negligence and apathy, with critics drawing attention to a perceived lack of urgency in dealing with a matter of life and death.

Even more distressing is the alleged disregard for Cleriston's deteriorating health, as highlighted in a medical report that went unheeded. The apparent oversight of this critical information further amplifies concerns about the adequacy of healthcare within the prison system and the responsiveness of the authorities to pressing matters.

Tragically, on November 20th, Cleriston Cunha succumbed to his circumstances inside the prison, marking a devastating outcome that underscores the gravity of the issues at hand. The incident has ignited debates about the ethical responsibility of the Brazilian state, the judiciary, and the federal senate in ensuring the safety and well-being of those in their custody.

The circumstances surrounding Cleriston's death demand a thorough and transparent investigation to shed light on any potential negligence, procedural flaws, or misconduct. This tragic event serves as a poignant reminder of the imperative to uphold justice and human rights, emphasizing the need for reform within the Brazilian legal system to prevent such heart-wrenching incidents from occurring in the future.
