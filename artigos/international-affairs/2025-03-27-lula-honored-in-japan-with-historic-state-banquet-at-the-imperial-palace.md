In a momentous gesture of diplomacy and cultural reverence, Japanese Emperor Naruhito and Empress Masako hosted a grand state banquet on March 25 at the Imperial Palace in Tokyo to honor Brazilian President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva and First Lady Rosângela da Silva. This marks the first state banquet held by the Japanese imperial family in six years, signifying the deepening ties between Japan and Brazil.

A historic aspect of the evening was the debut of Princess Aiko, daughter of the imperial couple, who joined such a formal occasion for the first time. Seated beside the President of Brazil’s Chamber of Deputies, her presence represented a symbolic generational continuity and reinforced Japan’s commitment to nurturing international friendships.

Among the approximately 110 distinguished guests were notable cultural figures including legendary footballer Miura Kazuyoshi and nipo-Brazilian singer Márcia, who together represent the enduring cultural bridge between the two nations.

Emperor Naruhito, in his address, expressed heartfelt hopes for the continued strengthening of bilateral relations:
**“I sincerely hope that, through the activities of the Japan-Brazil Friendship Exchange Year, the friendly relationship between our countries will deepen even further.”**

The banquet was carefully curated to reflect a harmonious blend of Japanese tradition and diplomatic elegance. While state banquets in Japan traditionally feature French cuisine, this occasion broke precedent by offering a Japanese appetizer—a first in such ceremonies. Moreover, traditional Edo Kiriko glasses were used to serve sake, adding a refined cultural touch to the celebration.

The ceremony underscored not only the formal state-level relations but also the cultural and emotional ties between Brazil and Japan—countries united by over a century of migration, cooperation, and shared values.

President Lula’s state visit, celebrated with imperial grandeur, highlights the importance both nations place on friendship, diplomacy, and mutual respect in an increasingly interconnected world.
